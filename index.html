My Favourite Trip!

One of the best and memorable trips that I've had was when I went on a trip with my friends to Goa. Goa is a state on the southwestern coast of India and its capital is Panaji. The official language, which is spoken by a majority of its inhabitants, is Konkani. Goa is visited by a large number of international and domestic tourists every year for its white sand beaches, active nightlife, places of worship and World Heritage-listed architecture. It also has rich flora and fauna as it lies on the Western Ghats range.

The idea of a trip came up one summer evening when we were all bored and didn't have anything to do. My group of friends and I were just sitting in our backyard and looking out for places where we could possibly go for a holidy and we thought "Goa" the land of beaches would be the perfect vacation spot for us to go. We were all excited, took permission from our parents and off we went on a four day trip. We were travelling by train and you know you've reached when you see your train passing next to the famous Dudhsagar Falls.

Below are the top 10 places that we visited along with a short description about the significance of the place and why it's famous.

DUDHSAGAR

Dudhsagar ('Sea of Milk') is a four-tiered waterfall located on the Mandovi River in the Indian state of Goa. This waterfall is also known as Tambdi Surla to some of the local people. There has been a lot of hype of Dudhsagar trek being closed for the general public. The Dudhsagar railway trek has been officially closed for the general public but the trek to the Dudhsagar falls bottom is still open for all.

FORT AGUADA

Fort Aguada is a well-preserved seventeenth-century Portuguese fort, along with a lighthouse, standing in Goa overlooking the Arabian Sea. Built in 1612, it was once the grandstand of 79 cannons.It has the capacity of storing 2,376,000 gallons of water, one of the biggest freshwater storage of the time in whole of Asia. The lower part served as a safe berth for Portuguese ships. The upper part acted as fort and watering station,has a moat, underground water storage chamber, gunpowder room, lighthouse, and bastions, it also has a secret escape passage to use during time of war and emergency. The lighthouse at the initial stage is used to emit light once in 7 minutes."

BASILICA OF BOM JESUS

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is a Roman Catholic basilica located in Goa and holds the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier. Bom Jesus' literally, 'Good (or Holy) Jesus') is the name used for the Ecce Homo in the countries of Portuguese colonization. The Jesuit church is India's first minor basilica, and is considered to be one of the best examples of baroque architecture and Portuguese Colonial architecture in India. It is one of the Seven Wonders of Portuguese Origin in the World. The remains of the saint still attract a huge number of tourists from all over the world,especially during the public viewing of his body every ten years.

PALOLEM BEACH

Beaches play a starring role among the attractions in Goa, contributing to why it's one of the best places to visit in India. Nearly every tourist who visits the area spends a day at Palolem Beach, a crescent-shaped stretch of white sand that overlooks the Arabian Sea in south Goa. While simply hanging out and soaking up the scenery is a perfect way to enjoy this beautiful area, Palolem Beach also offers an array of activities to fill your time. Choose from kayaking, yoga classes, dolphin-sightseeing trips, and swimming. You can also spend the night in one of Palolem's rustic coco huts.

CRUISES IN GOA

To enjoy a gaming experience amidst the pleasant landscape of Mandovi river, board on a cruise ship at Panaji for a refreshing day at the casino cruise in Goa. They offer the mesmerizing vistas of Arabian Sea and also revisit the historical remnants of Goa, most prominent being the Adil Shah's palace and Fort Aguada as you relax under the sun. It engages the tourists in a lively experience with games like American Roulette, Blackjack, Rummy, Stud Poker,Baccarat, Pontoon, and Flash. Other features of this activity include an entertainment night, delicious dinner and amazing performances by professional groups. The singing, dancing and other activities are a great way to enjoy with friends and family.

MANGESHI TEMPLE

Mangeshi temple is one of the largest and most frequently visited temples in Goa. The 450-year-old Shri Mangesh temple dedicated to the Hindu God Shiva stands out with its simple and yet exquisitely elegant structure. The temple architecture consists of several domes, pilasters and balustrades. It has a spacious hall which can accommodate over 500. The decor includes the chandeliers of the nineteenth century.There is a prominent Nandi Bull and a beautiful seven-story deepstambha (lamp tower), which stands inside the temple complex. The temple also has a magnificent water tank, which is believed to be the oldest part of the temple.

LAMGAU CAVES

One of the ‘hidden’ Goa tourist places, Lamgau Caves are made up of two man-made caves which were meant to be a residence for monks. They are of great importance to the Hindu community in Goa and they reflect the transition from Buddhism to Hinduism. The caves are designed in a Buddhist fashion but the idols of Nandi and the Shivaling outside the main entrance of the smaller of the two caves reveal another aspect. The scenic route up to the caves is another reason to visit them. The caves are located inside a dense areca nut plantation while trekking up to them you pass several small Goan villages on your way which are good and hospitable resting spots during the trek.

BONDLA WILDLIFE SANCTUARY

Bondla Wildlife Sanctuary is located in northeastern Goa. It is a popular destination for both tourists and schoolchildren. A wide variety of animal life can be encountered, including: sambar deer, Indian bison, Malabar giant squirrel, Indian peafowl and many species of snakes. Bondla provides sanctuary to leopards who have been injured in human-wildlife conflict, as well as "dancing" bears and cobras who, along with their trainers, need a new life after this treatment of endangered wildlife. Bondla zoo is known for its successful breeding of gaur. As it is the only zoo in Goa, many visit the zoo. The zoo provides an excellent environment to breed and do researches on animals

BENZ CELEBRITY WAX MUSEUM

The Benz Celebrity Wax Museum is an exciting interactive visitor attraction taking you on a journey through wax figure, the exceptional life size wax characters. The unique feature of the statues is the liveliness of the sculpted image, Background has been given to each statue to support and compliment to its celebrated history. This feature has made it one of its kinds Museum and this enhances the wax figure and makes it feel larger than life. For the visitors of the city and abroad this shall be a stupendous experience to feel and see the wax replicas of their favourite icons.

DONA PAULA JETTY

The Lovers Paradise – as it is called, Dona Paula Jetty is nestled on a rocky, hammer shaped headland. This idyllic rocky tourist attraction lies at the spot where the Mandovi and the Zuari rivers meet the Arabian Sea. It provides a magnificent view of the Mormugao harbor. Tranquil and blue, Dona Paula unravels the ultimate in aquatic sport and fun. The Dona Paula monument is located on a small islet, linked to the mainland by a small bridge and a quay where there are twin sculptures of a man and a woman on the rocks at the Dona Paula jetty.
